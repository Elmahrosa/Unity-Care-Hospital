# Sensitivity Analysis (12‑Month Cash Flow)

## Base Case (Reference)
- Ownership deal Month 6 ($250K)
- License deals Month 9 and Month 11 ($75K each)
- End‑year cash ≈ $300K–$350K

---

## Scenario A: 3‑Month Delay (Ownership closes Month 9)
- Cash becomes tight before Month 9.
- Month 9 inflow: Ownership ($250K) + License ($75K) = $325K.
- Year‑end cash ≈ $280K–$310K.

**Conclusion:** survivable with cost discipline; avoid discretionary spend until deal closes.

---

## Scenario B: 6‑Month Delay (Ownership closes Month 12)
- Seed is effectively exhausted by Month 11 without mitigation.
- Month 12 inflow: Ownership ($250K) + License ($75K) + maintenance (~$1.2K) ≈ $326K.

**Conclusion:** survival depends on strict controls (cost deferrals, reduced headcount, or bridge plan).

---

## Scenario C: No Ownership Deal in Year 1
- Only 2 license deals = $150K.
- Burn ≈ $310K–$330K.

**Conclusion:** not survivable without restructuring or fundraising.

---

## Strategic Implications
- Ownership deal is the **primary survival driver**.
- License deals alone are insufficient at this burn level.
- If Ownership slips beyond Month 9, execute a **bridge plan** (cost cut + accelerated pipeline).
